FPV Rover

Introduction

This has been one of the biggest projects I worked on. I wanted to push myself and work on something more complicated than I am used to.

The rover has 2 microcontrollers on it, both ESP32's. An ESP32 with a built in camera acts as the eye as well as hosting the webserver used to control it. The second one controls the motor drivers for the 6 wheel drive. The rover features rocker-boogie suspension to allow for it to driver over rough terrain.

Development

Going into the project, I had no idea how to host a webserver on a microcontroller, but I thought it would be the best way to wirelessly control it with my computer and allow me to view the camera feed. While teaching myself how to achieve this, I also struggled with the mechanical design. After much iteration and though, I finally achieved an outcome I am happy with.

Outcomes

I am happy with the outcome of the project, though there are countless places for improvement. I am able to control the rover via my laptop and view the live feed from the camera. Some minor stability issues occur in the stream, but the feed remains useable.

There are many improvements that can be made. The electronics and mechanical design could be simplified by removing 2 of the wheels and having only 4 wheel drive. The rover is not heavy enough to require all 6 wheels. The wheels could also be improved by adding a grippy material, but I did not posses the means to fabricated that at the time.
